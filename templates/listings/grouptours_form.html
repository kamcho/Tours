Tour Video Upload Guide

Best practices and tips for creating engaging tour videos

Best Practices

1

Keep it Short & Engaging

Aim for 2-5 minutes. Start with a hook and maintain viewer interest throughout.

2

Show, Don't Just Tell

Use visuals of destinations, activities, and experiences rather than just talking about them.

3

Include Key Information

Highlight unique selling points, what's included, and what makes your tour special.

4

Professional Quality

Use stable shots, good lighting, and clear audio. Consider using a tripod or stabilizer.

Content Structure

Opening Hook (0-15s)

Start with an exciting moment or stunning visual to grab attention immediately.

Tour Overview (15s-1min)

Brief introduction to the tour, destinations, and what travelers can expect.

Key Highlights (1-3min)

Showcase the most exciting activities, beautiful locations, and unique experiences.

Call to Action (3-5min)

End with a clear invitation to book and contact information.

Technical Requirements

File Format & Size

  • • Supported formats: MP4, MOV, AVI, WebM
  • • Maximum file size: 100MB
  • • Recommended resolution: 1080p (1920x1080)
  • • Aspect ratio: 16:9 (landscape) or 9:16 (portrait)

Audio & Video Quality

  • • Video bitrate: 2-5 Mbps for 1080p
  • • Audio: Clear narration, background music optional
  • • Frame rate: 24-30 fps for smooth playback
  • • Avoid shaky footage and poor lighting

Pro Tips

Use B-roll footage to show different angles and perspectives of destinations and activities.

Include captions or text overlays to highlight key information and make videos accessible.

Tell a story - show the journey from start to finish, not just random clips.

Use music that matches the mood and pace of your tour for better engagement.
